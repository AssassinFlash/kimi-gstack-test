## What makes a metal screen "decorative"

A decorative metal screen is a panel whose pattern, material, and finish do the visual work, unlike a solid partition or a plain guardrail. The pattern comes from laser cutting, CNC punching, or expanded metal mesh. The material is usually aluminum, stainless steel, brass, or corten (weathering steel). The finish ranges from powder coating and anodizing to brushed or mirror polish. Every choice in that chain changes how the screen reads in the room, so we treat pattern, material, and finish as one specification, not three.

## Common use 1: Interior partitions and space zoning

**The scenario.** An open lobby, a restaurant floor, or an open-plan office needs to feel like more than one zone, but a solid wall would kill the openness and block the light.

**Why a metal screen works.** A laser-cut screen reads as a wall from one angle and as air from another. It separates without enclosing. Foot traffic and sightlines pass through the pattern, so the space stays connected while each zone keeps its own identity. In a hotel lobby, a screen behind the seating area gives arrivals a sense of destination without a door. In a restaurant, a row of screens between the bar and the dining room lets noise and light bleed through while keeping the two experiences distinct.

**How we make it.** For interior partitions we usually recommend aluminum for its light weight and clean powder-coated finish, or stainless steel where the designer wants a metallic sheen. Panels are cut to the architect's pattern or to one of our standard geometries, then mounted in a demountable frame so the partition can be repositioned or removed if the layout changes. Standard panel sizes keep the price down; custom sizes and bespoke patterns are no problem when the design calls for them. Lead time for a typical interior partition order runs two to four weeks from approved drawings.

> **Specify this use if your project needs:** zone separation without losing light or openness, a feature element that doubles as a divider, or a way to break up a large open floor without drywall.

## Materials and processes (quick reference)

**Materials**
- Aluminum: light, powder-coated or anodized, best for large panels and ceilings.
- Stainless steel: brushed or mirror polish, structural and high-end.
- Brass and copper: premium, develops a patina over time.
- Corten (weathering steel): rusted finish for outdoor and industrial interiors.

**Processes**
- Laser cutting: most flexible, any pattern from a CAD file.
- CNC punching: repeat patterns, cost-effective for large runs.
- Expanded metal mesh: industrial look, strong, self-supporting.

**Sizes and customization**
Standard panel sizes ship fastest. Custom sizes and bespoke patterns are cut from your DWG or PDF. Send us the drawing and we will quote within one business day.
